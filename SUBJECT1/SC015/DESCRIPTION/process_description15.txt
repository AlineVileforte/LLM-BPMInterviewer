This process represents a supply chain and procurement workflow used to manage inventory replenishment, purchase order approval, supplier communication, and supplier payment activities. The process starts by checking the current inventory levels to determine whether stock quantities have fallen below the minimum threshold.

If inventory levels exceed the minimum threshold, the process ends without additional procurement actions. If inventory levels fall below the minimum level, the organization constructs a purchase order and submits it for review.

During the review stage, the purchase order may either be approved or rejected. Rejected purchase orders return to the inventory verification stage, creating a correction and reprocessing cycle. Approved purchase orders continue with financial and supplier-related activities.

Once approved, the process reserves financial funds and sends the purchase order to the supplier. The organization then matches the order and executes parallel supplier communication activities, including sending the packing slip and sending the invoice.

After all supplier documentation activities are completed and the financial reservation is confirmed, the process receives confirmation, releases the reserved funds, and finalizes the procurement cycle.
