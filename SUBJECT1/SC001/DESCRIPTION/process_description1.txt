This process represents the controlled distribution of banking access credentials and customer account materials. The process begins when the bank sends the customer’s PIN by mail. After a mandatory waiting period of two days, the bank sends the TANs to the customer. Then, after another two-day interval, the bank sends the customer’s bank card.
The process contains sequential mailing activities and time-based control events to guarantee that sensitive banking credentials are delivered in a secure and predefined order. The customer participates in the process by receiving the PIN, TANs, and card through message interactions between the bank and the customer.
