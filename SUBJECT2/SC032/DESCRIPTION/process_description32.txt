**Industrial Product Development** represents a complex industrial product development process that coordinates engineering, manufacturing, procurement, and production-readiness activities to transform an approved product concept into a production-ready solution. The process begins with concept approval and structural component development, followed by parallel efforts involving supplier negotiations, prototype production, circuit and sensor design, and cost and logistics analysis. In parallel, embedded software is developed and validated, production resources are prepared, and inspections are performed. Depending on the evaluation results, technical drawings may require modification before proceeding to testing and validation. The process continues with mechanical testing, final product validation, technical documentation consolidation, training material preparation, and authorization for large-scale production. The process concludes after all engineering, operational, and production-readiness activities have been completed and integrated, confirming the product is ready for industrial-scale manufacturing.
