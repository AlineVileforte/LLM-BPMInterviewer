This process represents an inventory resupply and supplier payment workflow used to replenish stock when inventory levels reach or fall below the minimum threshold. The process starts with checking the current inventory level to determine whether a new purchase order is required.

If the inventory level is above the minimum threshold, the process ends without additional actions. If the inventory level is at or below the minimum level, a purchase order is created and reviewed. Depending on the review result, the purchase order may either be accepted or rejected. Rejected purchase orders are reworked and sent back for another review cycle.

When the purchase order is accepted, the process simultaneously reserves purchase funds and sends the purchase order to the supplier. After the supplier interaction, the organization receives the order packing slip and the supplier invoice in parallel. Both documents are then recorded before the process continues. Finally, the reserved funds are released and the payment is issued to the supplier, completing the procurement and payment cycle.
